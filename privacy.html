Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026 · Runnr (“we”, “our”, or “us”)

Runnr is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and share your information when you use our running and fitness tracking app.

1. Data we collect

  • Account information: Email address and display name when you create an account or sign in (email and password, Google Sign-In, or Sign in with Apple). A user identifier is assigned by our authentication provider.
  • Run and fitness data: Distance, duration, pace, calories, route/GPS points, mode settings, notes you add to runs, effort, and related statistics.
  • Precise location: GPS location while you are actively recording a run (or using features that need your position on the map). We do not continuously track your location when you are not using those features.
  • Profile and preferences: Units, goals, in-run alerts, map style, game-mode settings, and similar preferences you configure in the app.
  • Optional profile metrics: Information you choose to enter (for example height, weight, or goals) if you save it in the app.
  • Leaderboard-related data: If you are signed in, we may publish aggregated statistics and a display name you provide to a public leaderboard dataset so other users can see rankings. Details stored are limited to what the app needs for that feature (for example distances, steps or game scores for time periods, and an optional display name).
  • Diagnostics: Crash logs and related diagnostic information sent through Firebase Crashlytics (operated by Google) to help us find and fix bugs.
  • Device information: Basic technical information (for example device model and OS version) as part of diagnostics and normal app operation.

2. How we use your data

  • To provide run tracking, maps, statistics, goals, and optional game modes.
  • To authenticate you, keep your account in sync when you are signed in, and restore your data on a new device.
  • To operate leaderboards and social-style features you choose to use.
  • To show weather for your area during a run when that feature is used (approximate location may be sent to a weather provider for that request).
  • To improve stability and performance using crash and diagnostic reports.

3. Where data is stored

  • Signed out: Core run and preference data may be stored only on your device.
  • Signed in: Your account, runs, preferences, and related content are stored in Google Firebase (including Cloud Firestore) so they can sync with your account. Diagnostic data may be processed through Firebase Crashlytics.
  • Premium access: Subscription purchases are handled by Apple. Information about whether your account has premium access may be stored on your device (for example in secure storage) as described in the app.

4. Third-party services

We rely on service providers that process data on our behalf or as part of providing the app. They have their own policies:

  • Firebase / Google (authentication, cloud database, crash reporting): Firebase Privacy
  • Google Sign-In: Google Privacy Policy
  • Sign in with Apple: Apple Privacy Policy
  • Map tiles (for example OpenStreetMap-based or CARTO raster tiles): map providers may receive standard technical requests (such as IP address) when tiles load; see their respective terms and policies.
  • Weather (Open-Meteo or similar): coordinates you use for weather may be sent to obtain a forecast.

5. Retention and deletion

  • We retain account and synced data until you delete your account or delete specific content where the app allows it.
  • You may delete your account and associated cloud data using the in-app account deletion flow where available. Local data may be removed using reset options in the app as described there.

6. Your choices

  • You can control location permission in your device settings. Without location access during a run, route recording may not work as intended.
  • You can sign out to stop using account-linked sync on that device.
  • You can contact us for privacy-related requests using the email below.

7. Children

Runnr is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.
